# H10 Stage 2 Phenotype Curriculum Results

Experiment family:

- `H10_STAGE2_NESTED_PHENOTYPE_CURRICULUM_20260715`
- `H10_STAGE2B_EARLY_STOP_BEFORE_HARD_REPLAY_20260715`
- `H10_STAGE2C_CLASS_MASS_PRESERVING_CURRICULUM_20260715`

Date completed: 2026-07-15

## Decision

`CLOSE_PHENOTYPE_ROLE_SAMPLING`

The revised difficulty definition was substantially more defensible than the old
in-sample easy/medium/hard score, but it was not a reliable training-weight target.
The full nested curriculum significantly reduced internal forced-classification
performance. Removing hard replay recovered much of the loss but still did not match
the source-free risk-balanced baseline. Preserving exactly equal low/high sampling
mass also failed.

No further role-factor or curriculum-stage search is allowed on these folds. The
derived roles remain useful for error stratification only.

## Answer to the design questions

1. Treating the three batches as one same-institution cohort was correct for this
   internal-capability experiment. Batch/source was absent from split construction,
   sampling, loss, model inputs, and checkpoint selection.
2. Difficulty could not be represented by a single confidence score. Physician
   phenotype plus nested model stability separated stable anchors, learnable
   boundaries, phenotype mimics, canonical failures, and sparse cases.
3. These categories were still context-dependent. Only 327/591 cases kept the same
   role in all three outer-training contexts.
4. More exposure to difficult cases was harmful. Stopping before hard replay improved
   BAcc from 0.7498 to 0.7780, but the unmodified risk-balanced baseline remained best
   at 0.7888.

## Integrity

- Cohort: 591 cases, treated as one internal hospital cohort.
- Split: subtype-only fivefold, SHA-256
  `f13030d7467c907851ed89abe14e385e024fcdddb6ad4be26433bc7994a10beb`.
- Every outer-training role was generated by three inner-fold OOF teachers restricted
  to that outer-training partition.
- Outer validation/test images and labels were unavailable to role generation.
- Physician concepts came only from the gross-observation field. Diagnosis text was
  forbidden.
- Physician concepts and roles were not model inputs and are not needed at inference.
- Model, six dense views, frozen encoder, optimizer, seed, threshold 0.5, and complete
  coverage were locked.
- Strict external data were not accessed.

## Why the revised roles still failed

The inner teachers themselves were only moderately accurate:

| Inner teacher sampler | Weighted inner BAcc |
| --- | ---: |
| Natural | 0.7464 |
| Risk-balanced | 0.7426 |
| Subtype-tempered | 0.7541 |

Across the three outer-training contexts available for each case:

- 327 cases had one stable role;
- 226 moved between two roles;
- 38 had three different roles;
- pairwise role agreement was 0.6808.

Thus the role was partly a property of which neighboring cases happened to be in the
training partition, not a fixed biological difficulty label.

For the full curriculum, post-hoc consensus-role outcomes were:

| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Canonical anchor | 107 | 0.9439 | 0.8879 | 2 | 8 | -6 |
| Learnable boundary | 115 | 0.6783 | 0.5739 | 9 | 21 | -12 |
| Persistent canonical failure | 12 | 0.2500 | 0.1667 | 0 | 1 | -1 |

Hard replay produced only a one-case net gain in persistent phenotype mimics while
destroying substantially more stable and boundary decisions. Training loss approached
zero as validation and test performance fell, which is consistent with memorization
of context-specific difficult cases.

## Phase and calibration diagnostics

Mean validation peaks across Stage 2 folds were:

- anchor warmup: 0.8017;
- boundary bridge: 0.8140;
- targeted replay: 0.7988;
- eligible checkpoint after at least eight replay epochs: 0.7925.

Forcing replay reduced the selected validation peak by 0.0290 on average. Stage 2B
therefore correctly tested the user's proposal to stop before more difficult exposure.
It recovered performance but did not beat the baseline.

Role factors also changed effective high-risk weight mass to 0.5204 on average during
the bridge. Validation-only threshold selection was unstable (fold thresholds
0.35-0.69) and reduced pooled Stage 2B BAcc from 0.7780 to 0.7560. Stage 2C then fixed
class mass at exactly 0.5/0.5 during training and still fell to 0.7628. Neither
post-hoc threshold tuning nor class-prior correction explains away the failure.

## Source audit only

Stage 2B BAcc was 0.8215/0.7500/0.7566 for batch1/batch2/third batch. Stage 2C was
0.7522/0.7321/0.7717. These labels were not used in training. Neither variant passed
the source-stability gate.

## Final interpretation

The revised phenotype/model-stability decomposition is useful for understanding the
591 cases, but it should not be converted into per-case curriculum weights. Persistent
failure mixes overlapping gross morphology, missing visual evidence, limited sample
coverage, and possibly unstable labels. Upweighting that mixture does not teach a
cleaner boundary.

The best directly comparable source-free internal classifier remains H10
risk-balanced at BAcc 0.7888 and ordinary accuracy 0.7986. The historical H3 mixed
source-fold result around BAcc 0.8003 used a different split/sampler and is not a
like-for-like improvement. A 90% full-coverage base classifier has not been obtained.

Future fixed-data experiments must change the visual evidence or representation, not
rename or resample the same difficult cases. H10 roles may be used to report subgroup
errors and to design representation-level hypotheses, but not as direct sampling
weights, inference features, confidence gates, or test-time thresholds.
